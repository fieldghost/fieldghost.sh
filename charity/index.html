Charity & Open-source

The tools we use every day are built on the backs of unpaid maintainers, and the peace we enjoy is not a reality for everyone.

If you have the means, please consider supporting the humanitarian efforts and open-source foundations listed below.

Humanitarian Aid

United24

The official fundraising platform of Ukraine. Protecting and rebuilding the nation.

Save the Children (Gaza)

Providing emergency aid, psychosocial support, and protection for children caught in the conflict.

UNICEF (Gaza)

Delivering critical humanitarian aid, clean water, and medical supplies to children in Gaza.

UNRWA

The United Nations Relief and Works Agency providing frontline assistance to Palestine refugees.

Open-Source Foundations

Free Software Foundation

Campaigning for computer user freedom and defending the rights of all software users.

FSFE

Empowering users to control technology and campaigning for Free Software in Europe.

Software Freedom Conservancy

Providing a non-profit home and infrastructure for Free, Libre, and Open Source Software projects.

Software in the Public Interest

Helping open-source organizations handle administrative tasks so they can focus on code.

GNOME Foundation

Supporting the development of the GNOME desktop environment and a free software computing ecosystem.

KDE e.V.

Supporting the KDE community in creating free and open-source software like the Plasma desktop.

Apache Software Foundation

Providing software for the public good and supporting hundreds of open-source projects.
